Research Projects

Major research projects related to renewable energy, smart grids, artificial intelligence, optimization, and power systems.

1. Novel Controller Design for Grid Connected Wind Turbines

This project focuses on improving the power quality and fault ride-through capability of grid-connected wind energy conversion systems (WECS). Advanced control strategies including PI controllers, nonlinear controllers, MSTFOTSMC, and STATCOM integration were investigated for DFIG-based systems. The research analyzed:

  • Voltage sag and harmonic distortion
  • Low Voltage Ride Through (LVRT)
  • Rotor Side Converter (RSC) control
  • Grid Side Converter (GSC) stabilization
  • 3L-NPC converter performance
  • Three-phase fault behavior
  • Power quality enhancement
Simulation studies were performed using MATLAB/Simulink to validate the proposed controller performance under balanced and unbalanced fault conditions.

2. AI-Based Optimal Integration of Renewable Distributed Energy Resources

This project investigates the optimal integration of renewable distributed generation resources including solar photovoltaic systems, wind turbines, capacitor banks, and battery energy storage systems into electrical distribution networks using Artificial Intelligence techniques. The research includes:

  • Advanced Multi-Objective Particle Swarm Optimization (MOPSO) for renewable DG allocation and sizing.
  • Voltage stability improvement and power loss minimization.
  • Voltage dependent load models including ZIP load analysis.
  • Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) optimization for high renewable penetration networks.
  • IEEE 12, IEEE 33, IEEE 69 and practical Pakistan feeder analysis.
The proposed optimization framework achieved significant reductions in system power losses and improvements in voltage profile stability.

3. Autonomous Distribution Network for Power Management

This project presents an autonomous microgrid and distribution network management strategy under islanded operating conditions. The research focuses on:

  • Optimal placement of DGs and capacitor banks
  • Jaya Algorithm based optimization
  • Power mismatch handling in islanded networks
  • Load shedding and energy management strategies
  • Reactive and active power optimization
  • Microgrid operational stability
The proposed analytical approach allows effective utilization of distributed energy resources during grid outages and maintenance conditions while minimizing power losses.

4. Techno-Economic Optimization of Renewable EV Charging Stations

This project investigates stand-alone renewable energy-based electric vehicle charging stations using techno-economic optimization techniques. Hybrid systems involving:

  • Solar PV systems
  • Wind turbines
  • Hydrogen fuel cells
  • Battery energy storage
were analyzed using HOMER Pro software. The study compared:
  • PV-Hydrogen systems
  • PV-Hydrogen-Battery systems
Results demonstrated that hybrid PV-Hydrogen-Battery systems provide better technical reliability and economic performance for EV charging applications.

5. Computational Investigation of Novel 2D Materials for Hydrogen Storage

This project explores hydrogen storage capabilities of novel 2D materials including hexagonal boron nitrides (h-BN) and transition metal nitrides (TMN) using computational techniques. The study utilized:

  • Density Functional Theory (DFT)
  • Ab-initio Molecular Dynamics (MD)
  • Li-doped h-BN monolayer analysis
  • Hydrogen adsorption/desorption investigation
The computed desorption temperature and stability analysis revealed that Li-doped h-BN is a promising and thermally stable material for future hydrogen storage applications.
